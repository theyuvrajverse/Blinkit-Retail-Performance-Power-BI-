# Blinkit Retail Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

## Business Problem

Retail businesses must continuously monitor sales performance, customer satisfaction, product demand, and outlet efficiency to maximize profitability. This project analyzes Blinkit's retail operations to identify the factors driving revenue across outlet locations, outlet types, and product categories.

The objective was to uncover actionable insights that can support inventory planning, outlet expansion strategies, and product portfolio optimization.

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains sales, product, and outlet information across multiple Blinkit retail locations.

Key dimensions analyzed:

* Outlet Location Tier
* Outlet Type
* Outlet Size
* Item Type
* Fat Content
* Customer Ratings
* Sales Performance

## Dashboard Metrics

* Total Sales: $1.20M
* Total Items Sold: 8,523
* Average Customer Rating: 3.9
* Multiple outlet types and location tiers analyzed

## Key Findings

* Total sales reached $1.20M across 8,523 products sold.
* Tier 3 locations generated the highest revenue at $472.13K, outperforming Tier 2 ($393.15K) and Tier 1 ($336.40K).
* Supermarket Type1 outlets contributed $787.55K in sales, accounting for the majority of total revenue.
* Grocery Stores, Supermarket Type2, and Supermarket Type3 generated substantially lower revenue compared to Supermarket Type1.
* Low Fat products consistently outperformed Regular products across all outlet tiers.
* Revenue concentration among specific outlet formats indicates strong operational differences between store categories.
* Customer ratings remained relatively stable with an average score of 3.9, suggesting generally positive customer satisfaction.

## Business Recommendations

* Prioritize expansion in Tier 3 markets due to their superior revenue performance.
* Increase investment in the Supermarket Type1 model, which demonstrates the strongest sales contribution.
* Expand inventory allocation for high-performing Low Fat product categories.
* Conduct deeper category-level analysis to identify additional high-growth product segments.
* Use outlet-type performance metrics to guide future store format decisions.
